what_i_learned: |
  Today, our team gave a practice presentation in front of our coordinator as
  preparation for the SAIRI Research Symposium on Sunday. Presenting to someone
  before the final event gave us the opportunity to receive valuable feedback
  on both our slides and our presentation skills.

  We were advised to make a few edits to some of our slides to improve clarity
  and organization. We also received tips on becoming more confident speakers,
  especially by talking naturally instead of relying too much on reading from
  the slides. This reminded me that an effective presentation is about engaging
  with the audience rather than simply reading information.

  Along with the constructive feedback, we also received many positive comments.
  Our coordinator complimented the overall quality of our slides, the way we
  presented our research, and the impact of the DermaBridge project. It was
  encouraging to hear that our hard work throughout the internship was clearly
  reflected in our presentation.

blockers: |
  There were no major technical blockers today. The only improvements we need
  to make are minor slide edits and continuing to practice our delivery before
  the final symposium presentation.

reflection: |
  Today's practice presentation boosted my confidence for Sunday's symposium.
  Receiving positive feedback while also learning where we can improve showed me
  that we are well prepared, but there is always room to become better
  presenters.

  It was especially rewarding to hear others recognize the value of our
  project. Knowing that our work on building a fair and accessible AI system for
  skin lesion screening resonates with others made all the effort we have put
  into this research feel worthwhile.

  We ended the day with a fun tie-dye activity alongside the other research
  groups. After weeks of research, coding, writing papers, and preparing
  presentations, it was a great opportunity to relax, be creative, and spend
  time with everyone before the final symposium. It was a nice reminder that
  this internship has been about building friendships and creating lasting
  memories just as much as it has been about research.
