what_i_learned: |
  Today began with an inspiring lecture by Chris Crawford on physiological
  computing. The session introduced us to Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) and
  explored how the technology has evolved over time. Before today, I knew very
  little about BCIs, but the lecture opened my eyes to how the human brain can
  interact directly with computers and the possibilities this technology holds
  for healthcare, accessibility, and human-computer interaction.

  During our research lab session, my team continued improving the DermaBridge
  mobile application. We used Claude to refine the user interface, making the
  design more polished and user-friendly. It was interesting to see how quickly
  we could iterate on UI ideas while still making sure the design aligned with
  the goals of our project.

  We also met with our mentor to finalize the machine learning models that will
  be used in our ensemble. We discussed the integration of the ensemble model
  into the mobile application and how the prediction pipeline will connect with
  the app's scanning feature. At this stage, we have a working application, and
  the remaining task is to verify that the complete workflow—from scanning an
  image to displaying the prediction results—functions correctly.

blockers: |
  I did not encounter any major blockers today. Our primary focus was refining
  the application and confirming that all of the different components are
  working together before final testing.

reflection: |
  Today was exciting because it felt like our project is finally coming
  together. After weeks of working on datasets, model training, literature
  reviews, and application development, we now have a functioning application
  that is close to demonstrating the complete user experience.

  I especially enjoyed learning about Brain-Computer Interfaces because it
  introduced me to an entirely new area of AI and computing that I had never
  explored before. It reminded me how broad the field of artificial
  intelligence is and how many opportunities there are to apply these
  technologies to solve real-world problems.

  We also took a short break from coding by playing Roblox together as a team.
  It was a fun way to relax, reconnect, and recharge before continuing our work
  on the final stages of the project. I am looking forward to testing the full
  application workflow and seeing our AI model successfully integrated into the
  app.
