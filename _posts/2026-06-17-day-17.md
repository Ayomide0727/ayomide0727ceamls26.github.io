what_i_learned: |
  Today, we trained our third deep learning model, EfficientNetV2, for our skin
  lesion classification project. The training process was divided into two
  phases. During the initial phase, we froze some of the model parameters and
  achieved an accuracy of approximately 25%. We discussed how freezing too much
  of the model could limit its ability to learn features specific to our dataset.

  In the second phase, we trained the model for 15 epochs and adjusted the
  training strategy by unfreezing the backbone and then re-freezing the bottom
  60%, allowing only the top 40% of the network to continue learning. This
  improved the model's performance and increased the accuracy to approximately
  36%.

  A large part of today's session focused on understanding why the model was not
  performing as well as expected. Instead of immediately changing more
  hyperparameters, we decided to step back and carefully examine our dataset.
  We visualized the training, validation, and test splits to ensure that each
  diagnosis category and skin tone group was properly represented across the
  datasets.

  Through this process, I gained a deeper understanding of model training and
  learned more about important machine learning concepts such as optimizers,
  overfitting, transfer learning, and hyperparameter tuning. Since this was our
  third model training experience, many of these concepts are becoming clearer
  to me.

  We also had a Zoom session with our faculty mentor, who gave a presentation on
  skin cancer. We learned about different types of skin cancer, how they appear,
  and some of the biological factors that contribute to their development.
