what_i_learned: |
  Today’s activities focused on understanding our research dataset, learning about
  artificial intelligence, and building AI-powered applications.

  During our Research Labs breakout session, my graduate mentor walked us through
  the dataset that will be used for our project. We discussed what the data contains,
  why it is important for our research, and how it can be analyzed. We also completed
  a hands-on activity in Google Colab where we learned how to read, explore, and
  better understand a dataset. This gave me a clearer understanding of the first
  steps involved in working with research data.

  Another highlight of the day was participating in Google AI Quests. The activities
  were interactive, visually appealing, and engaging. Through the quests, I learned
  how artificial intelligence can be used to solve real-world problems and how data
  is collected and prepared for AI systems. The experience made complex AI concepts
  easier to understand in a fun and practical way.

  In Python 102, we continued learning about Gradio and explored how it can be
  connected to a large language model. We used Grok as our model and learned more
  about how AI-powered applications can be built and deployed. This session helped
  me better understand how user interfaces can interact with AI models behind the scenes.
