what_i_learned: |
  Today, my teammates and I focused on improving the DermaBridge mobile
  application while our graduate mentor finalized the Methodology and Results
  sections of our research paper.

  We began by brainstorming the overall user experience of the app. We explored
  existing healthcare and dermatology applications to better understand what
  screens, features, and user flows should be included in our own application.
  This helped us identify ways to make DermaBridge more intuitive and easier to
  navigate.

  One of the biggest accomplishments today was implementing a new onboarding
  experience. We added a three-screen introduction that explains what
  DermaBridge does, our commitment to fairness across different skin tones, and
  how the app connects users with dermatology care. After completing the
  onboarding, users can choose to create an account, sign in, or continue as a
  guest.

  We also improved the overall look and feel of the application by introducing
  reusable UI components such as buttons, cards, and navigation elements. This
  created a more consistent design throughout the app while reducing duplicated
  code. We also implemented accessibility improvements by adding screen reader
  labels and improving navigation throughout the application.

  Another important milestone was implementing the camera flow logic. Although
  the machine learning model has not yet been integrated, we now have the basic
  capture workflow in place, bringing the application one step closer to a fully
  functional AI-powered skin lesion screening tool.

blockers: |
  I did not encounter any major technical blockers today. Most of the work
  focused on improving the application's user experience and preparing it for
  model integration.

reflection: |
  Today made me realize that building an AI application involves much more than
  creating an accurate machine learning model. User experience, accessibility,
  and thoughtful interface design are just as important if people are going to
  trust and use the application.

  Claude Code has become an incredibly helpful tool throughout the development
  process. After I started using Claude Code directly in my terminal, it became
  much easier to debug issues, understand unfamiliar parts of the codebase, and
  implement new features while still learning how everything fits together.

  Looking ahead, our mentor has finalized the two best-performing models and
  created an ensemble model. Our next major milestone will be integrating this
  ensemble into the mobile application. We also plan to film our project pitch
  video tomorrow after gathering the materials we need. I am excited to see our
  research continue evolving into a complete, user-facing application.
