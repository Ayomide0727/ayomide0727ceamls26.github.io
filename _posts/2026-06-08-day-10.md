what_i_learned: |
  Today, we worked in pairs on a mini-project focused on preparing and merging
  two skin lesion datasets, HAM10000 and PAD-UFES-20. The goal was to create a
  unified dataset that can be used for machine learning and skin cancer
  classification.

  Throughout the project, we learned how to load and inspect datasets, examine
  metadata, standardize diagnosis labels, and organize information such as age,
  sex, lesion location, and diagnosis. We also explored image preprocessing
  techniques, including normalizing image data and preparing it for machine
  learning models.

  One of the most important lessons from this activity was understanding how
  dataset quality affects model performance. We identified duplicate diagnosis
  labels caused by inconsistent naming conventions and discussed how class
  imbalance can influence evaluation metrics such as accuracy and F1 score.

  Our Professor gave us a lecture on skin cancer where we learned about the structure
  of the skin, common skin lesions, causes of skin cancer, warning signs, and
  the three major types of skin cancer: Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC), Squamous
  Cell Carcinoma (SCC), and Melanoma. I also learned about the ABCDE rule used
  to identify suspicious skin lesions.

blockers: |

reflection: |
