what_i_learned: |
  Today began with another literature review session led by Dr. Anita Pandey.
  The session focused on how to effectively conduct a literature review and
  organize a research paper. I learned the importance of selecting credible
  sources from databases such as Google Scholar, JSTOR, and PubMed, as well
  as how to identify research gaps and summarize prior studies.

  One concept that stood out to me was the structure of a research paper,
  including the title, abstract, introduction, background, methodology,
  discussion, conclusion, and references. I also learned what information
  should be included in an annotated literature review and how to critically
  evaluate research papers.

  During our research lab sessions, we continued exploring and preparing our
  skin lesion dataset. Today we focused on renaming columns to make the data
  easier to work with and discussed strategies for handling missing values.
  These preprocessing steps are important because they help ensure the dataset
  is clean and ready for machine learning tasks.

reflection: |
  Today's activities helped me better understand that successful AI research
  involves much more than building models. Learning how to evaluate research
  papers, identify gaps in existing work, and organize findings is essential
  for producing quality research.

  I also enjoyed seeing how data preprocessing plays a critical role in the
  machine learning pipeline. Small tasks such as renaming columns and handling
  missing values may seem simple, but they are necessary for building reliable
  and accurate models.
