what_i_learned: |
  During our research lab session, we learned about image augmentation and its
  importance in machine learning and computer vision. My graduate mentor introduced
  us to several image augmentation techniques, including horizontal flipping,
  vertical flipping, rotation, and Gaussian blur.

  We completed a hands-on activity where we applied each technique to an image and
  compared the augmented versions to the original image. This helped me understand
  how image augmentation can create additional training samples without collecting
  new data.

  My mentor explained that image augmentation is especially important for our
  research project because our dataset is imbalanced. For example, if there are
  fewer images representing darker skin tones, augmentation techniques can help
  increase the number of training samples available for those categories and improve
  model performance.

  Later, we had our Figma 101 check-in where we discussed the progress of our web
  application replication projects and received feedback on our designs.

  During Python 102, we continued working on the Gradio Game Lab worksheet. We
  focused on completing the structure of AI-powered games by connecting Gradio with
  Groq. Some of the projects included an AI-generated riddle game and an interactive
  escape story game that uses AI to generate responses and story progression.

blockers: |
  I did not face any major blockers today. The image augmentation concepts were new
  to me, but the hands-on activity helped me better understand how each technique
  affects an image and why these methods are useful in machine learning.

reflection: |
  The most interesting part of today was learning about image augmentation because
  it connected directly to our research project. Before today, I understood that
  datasets could be imbalanced, but I did not fully understand how researchers can
  address that problem without collecting entirely new data.

  Overall, today's activities helped me better understand both the machine learning
  concepts behind our research and the practical skills needed to build AI-driven
  applications.
