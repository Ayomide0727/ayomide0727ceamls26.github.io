what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a meeting with our graduate mentor, where each team member
  shared an update on the progress we made yesterday. During my update, I
  presented the concept of the Individual Typology Angle (ITA), which I
  discovered while researching skin tone representation in dermatology
  datasets. I explained how ITA could potentially be used to estimate skin tone
  for datasets that do not already include skin tone labels. My mentor thought
  it was an interesting idea and suggested that we investigate it further before
  deciding whether it could be incorporated into our project.

  After the meeting, we focused on completing the reference section of our
  research paper. My task was to locate the BibTeX citations for four of our
  research papers and transfer them into our Overleaf project. This helped me
  become more familiar with managing references and organizing citations in a
  collaborative research paper.

  I also continued exploring additional datasets for our project. Today, I
  downloaded the ISIC 2019 dataset and began inspecting its structure,
  annotations, and metadata to determine whether it could be useful for our
  research and whether it contains information related to skin tone or other
  relevant clinical features.

  After lunch, our faculty mentor gave a lecture on DNA structure. We learned
  about the organization of DNA, its role in genetics, and how genetic changes
  can contribute to diseases such as skin cancer. The session helped connect the
  biological foundations of skin cancer to the AI research we are conducting.

blockers: |
  The main challenge today was continuing the search for datasets that include
  reliable skin tone information. While I was able to begin exploring the ISIC
  2019 dataset, determining whether it meets the needs of our project will
  require further investigation.

reflection: |
  Today's work reminded me that research often involves exploring new ideas
  before knowing whether they will become part of the final project.
  Discovering ITA has made me more curious about fairness in medical AI, and I
  look forward to learning more about whether it can help address the lack of
  skin tone labels in existing datasets.

  I also enjoyed contributing to our research paper by organizing citations in
  Overleaf. Although it seems like a small task, I realized that properly
  managing references is an important part of producing a well-organized
  academic paper.

  Tomorrow, I plan to continue exploring the ISIC 2019 dataset and investigate
  whether it can strengthen our research by providing additional data for model
  training and evaluation.
