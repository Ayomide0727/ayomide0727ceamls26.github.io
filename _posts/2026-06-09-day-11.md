what_i_learned: |
  During our research lab session, our graduate mentor reviewed the skin lesion
  dataset mini-project that we completed earlier this week. She walked us through
  the preprocessing workflow and explained many of the machine learning concepts
  and terminology involved in preparing data for model training.

  At first, I struggled to fully understand some of the concepts, but after
  spending time learning about Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and data
  encoding techniques, many of the ideas became much clearer. I learned how
  categorical data can be converted into numerical formats using different
  encoding methods and why this step is important for machine learning models.

  I also learned about Logistic Regression, overfitting, and the structure of
  CNNs. We discussed how CNNs process images using convolution layers, activation
  functions, pooling layers, and fully connected layers. Additionally, I learned
  about max pooling and how it helps reduce computational complexity while
  preserving important image features.

  During the literature review session, we continued developing our understanding
  of how to evaluate research papers and identify information that can support
  our research project.

blockers: |
  My main challenge today was understanding some of the machine learning concepts
  and terminology discussed during the mini-project review. However, after reading
  more about CNNs, data encoding, and classification models, I was able to connect
  these concepts to our skin lesion classification project and gain a much better
  understanding.

reflection: |
  Today's session helped me see how many different components work together in a
  machine learning pipeline. Before today, I understood the general idea of
  training a model, but I had not fully appreciated the importance of data
  preprocessing, encoding, and model architecture.

  Learning about CNNs was especially valuable because they are widely used in
  computer vision tasks such as skin lesion classification. I feel more confident
  in my understanding of the technical concepts behind our project and look
  forward to applying this knowledge as we continue developing our models.
