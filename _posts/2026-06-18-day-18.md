what_i_learned: |
  Today, we continued working through the Kaggle Python for Data Science course.
  Much of the material served as a refresher on Python fundamentals, which helped
  reinforce concepts that are important for data analysis and machine learning.
  While I was already familiar with some of the topics, I am looking forward to
  reaching the more data science-focused sections of the course where we can work
  with datasets and apply Python to real-world problems.

  During our research lab session, we also finalized our weekly presentation
  slides. We reviewed the accomplishments, challenges, and progress we made this
  week and prepared to share our updates during the weekly showcase.

  One of the highlights of the day was attending the Summer Lecture Series
  presentation by Dr. Shaun Gittens. The session provided an overview of the
  evolution of AI-assisted coding, from early programming tools to modern AI
  systems, coding assistants, and AI agents. We discussed concepts such as
  prompt engineering, context engineering, AI workflows, and the growing role of
  agent-based systems in software development. The presentation offered valuable
  insight into how AI is changing the way developers write, test, and maintain
  code.
