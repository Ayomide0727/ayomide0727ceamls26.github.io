what_i_learned: |
  Today started with a visit to the venue where we will present our research
  during the SAIRI Symposium. We walked around the presentation space to
  familiarize ourselves with the layout, where our poster and presentation
  areas will be, and the overall environment. It was helpful to see the venue
  ahead of time because it made the upcoming presentation feel much less
  intimidating and allowed us to mentally prepare for the event.

  After touring the venue, we participated in the SAIRI Field Day. Everyone
  wore the tie-dye shirts we made earlier in the week, which made the event
  feel even more special. We competed in several team activities, including
  Capture the Flag, the Three-Legged Race, and the Blind Relay. These games
  required communication, teamwork, and trust, making them a fun way to spend
  time with the other research groups.

blockers: |
  There were no research or technical blockers today. The day was focused on
  preparing mentally for the symposium and enjoying time with everyone in the
  program before our final presentations.

reflection: |
  Field Day was one of the most enjoyable experiences of the internship. It was
  fun competing with my teammates, cheering each other on, and interacting with
  students from the other research groups. These moments reminded me that the
  SAIRI program has been about much more than research; it has also been about
  building friendships, developing teamwork, and creating memories that I will
  carry with me long after the internship ends.

  With the symposium just around the corner, I feel excited and ready to
  showcase everything our team has accomplished through the DermaBridge project.
