what_i_learned: |
  Today, I continued working on the mobile application for our DermaBridge
  project. After successfully running the app on my laptop yesterday, I was
  able to launch it using an Android emulator on my computer. This was an
  exciting milestone because it allowed me to test the application in a mobile
  environment without relying solely on my physical device.

  My next goal is to get the web version of the application running so that I
  can verify it is properly connected to the backend. The image scanning feature
  is partially working, and once the frontend and backend are fully connected,
  we will be able to begin integrating our trained machine learning models into
  the application.

  During our research lab session, we also presented our weekly progress to the
  other research groups. We shared what we accomplished this week, the
  challenges we encountered, and our plans for the coming week. Our roadmap
  includes implementing bias mitigation techniques using reweighing and
  Fairlearn post-processing, creating a model ensemble by combining
  EfficientNet-B0, ResNet50, and EfficientNetV2, integrating Grad-CAM
  visualizations and patient metadata into the application, exploring SHAP for
  feature importance, tuning prediction thresholds across different skin tone
  groups, and completing the Discussion and Conclusion sections of our research
  paper.

blockers: |
  I did not encounter any major blockers today. The primary focus was verifying
  that the application could run successfully in the Android emulator and
  planning the next steps for integrating the backend and machine learning
  models into the application.

reflection: |
  Today was encouraging because I can finally see the application taking shape.
  Running the app in an Android emulator made the project feel much closer to a
  working product rather than just a collection of code and models.

  Presenting our weekly progress also helped me realize how much work we have
  completed over the past several weeks and how much there is still to build.
  Looking ahead, I am especially excited about integrating our machine learning
  models into the application and implementing explainability features like
  Grad-CAM and SHAP, since these will make our AI system more transparent and
  trustworthy for users.
