what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a Data Analysis Workshop led by Dr. Jamell Dacon. During the
  session, we explored how different machine learning models can be applied to a
  breast cancer dataset. We trained and compared several classification models,
  including Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN),
  and discussed how each model performs on the same dataset.

  Dr. Dacon also explained how to interpret model evaluation metrics using the
  confusion matrix. I learned the meaning of true positives (TP), true negatives
  (TN), false positives (FP), and false negatives (FN), and why these metrics
  are important when evaluating classification models, especially in healthcare
  applications. We also practiced visualizing data to better understand patterns
  and model performance.

  During our research lab session, our mentor asked us to complete the Kaggle
  Python course to strengthen our programming skills. Afterward, we began
  working on our Mid-Summer Research Presentation by organizing our slides,
  summarizing our accomplishments, discussing the challenges we encountered, and
  highlighting the progress we have made on the DermaBridge project.

blockers: |
  I did not encounter any major blockers today. The workshop introduced several
  new evaluation concepts, but the hands-on examples helped me understand how
  they are used to assess machine learning models.

reflection: |
  Today's workshop helped me connect many of the machine learning concepts that
  we have been learning throughout the internship. Seeing multiple models trained
  on the same dataset made it easier to understand their strengths, weaknesses,
  and how evaluation metrics can influence model selection.

  I also enjoyed beginning our Mid-Summer Research Presentation because it gave
  me the opportunity to reflect on how much I have learned since the start of
  the program. Looking back at our progress has shown me how much my confidence
  has grown in machine learning, data preprocessing, model training, and AI
  research.
