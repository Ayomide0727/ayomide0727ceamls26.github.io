what_i_learned: |
  Today was one of our most hands-on and intensive research sessions so far.
  We successfully trained our first deep learning model, EfficientNet-B0, and
  began training a ResNet50 model for comparison.

  Throughout the training process, I learned how to read and interpret model
  performance using accuracy and loss graphs. I gained a better understanding
  of how these metrics can be used to evaluate whether a model is learning
  effectively and how they can help identify potential issues such as poor
  performance or overfitting.

  My graduate mentor also included several TODO sections within the code for us
  to complete. This allowed me to follow along with the implementation, gain
  hands-on experience, and better understand how the training pipeline works.
  Rather than simply running the code, I was able to actively contribute and
  learn the reasoning behind each step.

  During training, our first model did not achieve the results we expected.
  We investigated the issue and worked on adjusting hyperparameters to improve
  performance. This helped me understand how important hyperparameter tuning is
  in the machine learning process.

  We also took a break from coding and participated in a fun group activity on
  Gartic Phone. It was a great opportunity to relax, laugh with other interns,
  and recharge before returning to our research work.

blockers: |
  One challenge we encountered was that our initial model performance was not as
  strong as expected. However, this became a valuable learning experience as we
  explored different hyperparameter adjustments and discussed ways to improve
  the model's results.

reflection: |
  Today was challenging, but it was also one of the most rewarding days of the
  program so far. For the first time, I felt deeply involved in the model
  training process rather than just learning about machine learning concepts in
  theory.

  Being able to interpret training metrics, modify code, and troubleshoot model
  performance helped me gain confidence in my understanding of deep learning.
  Although training models can be complex, I enjoyed the challenge and felt
  excited seeing our work move closer to a functioning skin lesion
  classification system.
