what_i_learned: |
  Today, I shifted my focus from skin tone estimation to the development of our
  mobile application. After spending a considerable amount of time debugging the
  project, I was finally able to get the application running on my laptop. Using
  Claude Code within my IDE, I worked through several setup and configuration
  issues until the project successfully compiled.

  One of the highlights of the day was seeing the application run on my phone
  through Expo. It was exciting to move beyond working only with code and
  finally interact with a working version of the app. I also began preparing my weekly presentation slides by documenting the
  accomplishments, challenges, and findings from this week's research.

  Earlier this week, I made several attempts to create Fitzpatrick skin tone
  labels for additional datasets using ITA-based estimation. However, after
  multiple experiments, I was unable to achieve results that were reliable
  enough for our project. Rather than continuing to pursue an approach that was
  not producing satisfactory outcomes, I decided to temporarily shift my focus
  to the mobile application while we continue evaluating better methods for skin
  tone estimation.

blockers: |
  My biggest challenge today was debugging the mobile application environment.
  There were several dependency and configuration issues that prevented the app
  from running correctly. Solving these problems required a lot of trial and
  error, along with careful debugging.

  Another challenge was accepting that my current ITA-based skin tone estimation
  approach was not producing reliable results. While it was disappointing, it
  also helped me recognize when it is appropriate to pivot to another task and
  return with a fresh perspective later.

reflection: |
  Today was very rewarding because I finally reached a milestone that I had been
  working toward for several days. Seeing the application launch successfully on
  my phone through Expo made all of the debugging worthwhile.

  I also learned an important lesson about research and software development:
  not every idea works the first time. Sometimes the best decision is to step
  away from a difficult problem, focus on another part of the project, and come
  back later with new knowledge and a different approach.

  Moving forward, I am excited to continue contributing to the mobile
  application while also revisiting skin tone estimation methods when we have a
  stronger strategy for improving their accuracy.
