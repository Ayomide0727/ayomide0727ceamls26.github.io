what_i_learned: |
  Today, I had the opportunity to attend the AWS Summit in Washington, D.C. It
  was an exciting experience because I was able to explore how companies are
  using cloud computing and artificial intelligence to solve real-world
  problems.

  Throughout the day, I visited several company booths, including Datadog,
  GitLab, Commvault, AMD, and many others. I spoke with representatives about
  their technologies, internship opportunities, and career paths in software
  engineering and AI. I also collected some fun merchandise while learning more
  about the different companies.

  One of my personal goals for the event was to expand my professional network.
  Although I hoped to make 20 LinkedIn connections, I was able to connect with
  around 10 professionals and students from different universities and
  companies. Even though I did not reach my original goal, I was happy with the
  meaningful conversations I had and the connections I made.

  I also attended a breakout session titled **"Building an AI-Ready University:
  From Campus to Career Readiness."** The session focused on how universities
  can prepare students for an AI-driven future by integrating AI into teaching,
  research, and campus operations. I learned about the AI Readiness Maturity
  Model, which outlines the stages institutions can follow as they adopt AI,
  from experimental use to full institutional transformation. One of the key
  takeaways was that AI readiness is not simply about adopting new technology,
  but about creating a culture, governance structure, and learning environment
  that prepares students for the future workforce.

blockers: |
  I did not encounter any major challenges today. My only disappointment was not
  reaching my goal of making 20 LinkedIn connections. However, I realized that
  building meaningful professional relationships is more valuable than simply
  collecting a large number of contacts.

reflection: |
  Today was one of the most inspiring professional development experiences I
  have had during the internship. It was exciting to see so many companies
  showcasing how they are using AI and cloud technologies across different
  industries.

  The breakout session made me think about how universities can better prepare
  students for careers in artificial intelligence. As a computer science
  student, it reminded me that learning technical skills alone is not enough—we
  also need to understand how AI can be responsibly integrated into education,
  research, and the workplace.

  Overall, attending the AWS Summit motivated me to continue developing my
  technical skills, expand my professional network, and seek opportunities to
  apply AI to solve meaningful real-world problems.
