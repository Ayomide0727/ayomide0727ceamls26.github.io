  During our research lab session, our graduate mentor assigned us an individual
  task to help investigate why our skin lesion classification models have been
  underperforming. Each team member was assigned a dataset focused on a specific
  skin tone and asked to independently clean the data and retrain the three
  models we have been working with.

  While cleaning my dataset, I encountered an issue in the sex column. The data
  contained multiple versions of the same categories, including "male", "Male",
  "female", "Female", "unknown", and misspelled variations. When I attempted to
  apply label encoding, these inconsistencies resulted in incorrect values and
  missing entries.

  To solve the problem, I standardized the values using mapping techniques before
  applying encoding. I also handled missing data by imputing the median age for
  missing age values and assigning "unknown" to missing sex entries. By the end
  of the session, I had successfully cleaned and prepared my dataset for model
  training.
