what_i_learned: |
  During our research lab session, my graduate mentor gave us valuable insight into
  how to evaluate research papers and determine whether they are suitable for a
  literature review. We discussed important factors to consider when selecting
  papers, including relevance, credibility, and the quality of the research.

  We also talked about our observations from the data exploration activity. One
  observation I shared was that the dataset appeared to be imbalanced, meaning that
  some skin cancer categories had significantly more samples than others. This could
  potentially affect model performance by making it easier for the model to predict
  certain types of skin cancer while struggling with less represented classes.

  Later, Dr. Anita Pandey led a session on literature reviews. She explained the
  purpose of a literature review, how it contributes to the research process, and
  what researchers should look for when selecting papers. The presentation was very
  insightful and helped me better understand how to conduct an effective literature
  review for my own project.

  During Python 102, we continued working with Gradio and Groq to build AI-powered
  applications. The session provided more hands-on experience with integrating large
  language models into interactive applications.

blockers: |
  I did not face any major blockers today. However, I am still developing my ability
  to critically evaluate research papers and identify the most relevant sources for
  a literature review. Today's sessions helped me gain more confidence in this area.

reflection: |
  Today's sessions helped me understand that research is not only about building
  models but also about understanding existing work and identifying gaps in the
  literature. I found it especially interesting to analyze the dataset and discuss
  potential issues such as class imbalance, since these factors can significantly
  impact the performance and fairness of machine learning models.

  The literature review session was particularly valuable because it provided skills
  that I will use throughout my research project. I look forward to applying these
  techniques as I begin reviewing papers related to AI, skin cancer detection, and
  trustworthy machine learning.
