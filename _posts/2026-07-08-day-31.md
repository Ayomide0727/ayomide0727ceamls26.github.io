what_i_learned: |
  Today in the research lab, we focused on improving the quality of our research
  paper by completing and verifying the citations in both the Introduction and
  Literature Review sections. This helped ensure that our claims were properly
  supported by the existing research.

  I also made significant progress on my skin tone estimation task. I developed
  a manual ITA (Individual Typology Angle) computation pipeline and tested it on
  the DDI dataset by comparing the estimated skin tones to the dataset's
  existing skin tone labels. Although the pipeline achieved an accuracy of about
  41%, the other evaluation metrics were not very promising, suggesting that the
  current approach is not yet reliable enough for our project.

  Based on these results, I decided that my next step will be to complete the
  download of the Fitzpatrick17k dataset and train a skin tone estimation model
  following the STAR-ED approach that I recently discovered while reading
  research papers on skin tone estimation. I am excited to compare a
  machine-learning-based approach with the traditional ITA method.

  After lunch, we had SAIRI Connect where we spent time with the other research
  groups. We played games together and later watched the 2025 movie *Mercy*,
  which explores a future where an AI-powered judicial system is responsible for
  investigating crimes and making legal decisions.

blockers: |
  The biggest challenge today was seeing that my ITA pipeline did not perform as
  well as I had hoped. Although it was able to generate skin tone estimates, the
  overall performance showed that additional improvements or a different
  approach may be necessary before it can be used reliably.

reflection: |
  Today reminded me that not every experiment produces the results you expect,
  and that is an important part of research. Even though my ITA pipeline only
  achieved about 41% accuracy, I now have evidence that helps guide my next
  steps instead of simply guessing what might work.

  Watching *Mercy* was both exciting and thought-provoking. The AI system in the
  movie was incredibly effective at gathering evidence and making decisions, but
  it also raised important questions about privacy, trust, and how much control
  society should give to AI systems. It reminded me that as we continue
  developing AI technologies, technical performance must always be balanced with
  ethical considerations.

  Tomorrow, I plan to continue exploring the STAR-ED approach by training a
  skin tone estimation model on the Fitzpatrick17k dataset and comparing its
  performance with my current ITA pipeline.
