what_i_learned: |
  Today, we officially began writing our research paper for the DermaBridge
  project. We started developing the major sections of the paper, including the
  abstract, introduction, and literature review.

  To make the writing process more efficient, our research team was divided into
  pairs, with each group responsible for researching and writing different
  sections of the literature review. This allowed us to cover multiple topics
  and research papers while collaborating as a team.

  Through this process, I learned more about how research papers are structured
  and how each section contributes to communicating the purpose, motivation, and
  significance of a research project. I also gained experience reviewing
  scholarly articles and summarizing their key findings in an academic format.

blockers: |
  One challenge I faced was deciding how much information to include when
  summarizing research papers. It was important to balance providing enough
  detail while keeping the writing concise and focused.

reflection: |
  Today gave me a better appreciation for the amount of work that goes into
  writing a research paper. Beyond conducting experiments, researchers must also
  clearly communicate their ideas, justify their approach, and connect their
  work to existing studies.

  Working in pairs made the process more manageable and allowed us to learn from
  each other's perspectives. I am excited to continue developing the paper and
  seeing how all of the different sections come together into a complete
  research study.
