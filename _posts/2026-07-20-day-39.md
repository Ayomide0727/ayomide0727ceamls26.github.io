what_i_learned: |
  Today, my team dedicated the entire day to creating our final presentation
  slides for the SAIRI Summer Research Program. We organized our research into
  a clear and engaging presentation by documenting our research questions,
  methodology, dataset, model development, key findings, and the impact of our
  work on DermaBridge.

  As we built each section of the presentation, we discussed how to best
  communicate our research to different audiences. Rather than simply presenting
  technical details, we focused on telling the story of our project—from the
  healthcare problem we wanted to address, to the datasets we curated, the
  models we trained, and how everything comes together in the DermaBridge
  mobile application.

  Working through the presentation also helped reinforce everything we have
  accomplished throughout the internship and reminded me how much progress our
  team has made over the past several weeks.

blockers: |
  We did not encounter any major technical issues today. Our main challenge was
  deciding how to present a large amount of research in a concise, visually
  appealing, and easy-to-understand format without overwhelming our audience.

reflection: |
  Creating our final presentation made me reflect on the entire research
  journey. Looking back at our research questions, dataset creation, fairness
  analysis, model development, and application design showed me how each stage
  built upon the previous one to create a complete AI solution.

  I also realized that presenting research is a skill in itself. It's not just
  about showing results—it's about communicating the motivation, process, and
  impact in a way that others can understand and appreciate.

  As we approach the conclusion of the SAIRI program, I feel proud of how much
  our team has accomplished. I am looking forward to presenting our work and
  sharing everything we have learned throughout this research experience.
