what_i_learned: |
  Today marked the beginning of the in-person portion of the SAIRI Summer
  Research Program. We started with an orientation session where Michael
  reviewed the goals of the program, expectations for participants, and the
  requirements for our midterm and final research presentations.

  We also attended a hands-on Data Analytics session led by Dr. Dacon. During
  the session, we explored the fundamentals of data analysis and learned how
  data can be used to uncover patterns and support decision-making. The
  interactive format made it easier to understand the concepts and apply them
  in practice.

  Later, we participated in a presentation on AI ethics and bias. The session
  highlighted the importance of building responsible AI systems and discussed
  how bias can affect model performance and decision-making, especially in
  sensitive areas such as healthcare.

  One of the most enjoyable parts of the day was a team-building activity where
  each research group worked together to build the tallest structure possible
  using tape, spaghetti, and a marshmallow. The challenge encouraged teamwork,
  creativity, and problem-solving.

  We ended the day by visiting our research lab and becoming more familiar with
  the environment where we will be conducting research throughout the remainder
  of the program.

blockers: |
  I did not encounter any major challenges today. Most of the day focused on
  orientation, learning, and getting acquainted with the in-person research
  environment.

reflection: |
  Today was one of my favorite days of the program so far because I finally had
  the opportunity to meet everyone in person. After spending several weeks
  working virtually, it was exciting to connect with my teammates, mentors, and
  other participants face-to-face.

  The team-building activity was a great way to break the ice and strengthen our
  collaboration skills. I also enjoyed learning about AI ethics because it
  reinforced the importance of building fair and responsible AI systems.

  Overall, I am excited to begin the in-person phase of the program and look
  forward to working more closely with my team as we continue developing our
  research project.
